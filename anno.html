Who Is Hideaki Anno?

Hideaki Anno is a Japanese animator, film director, and screenwriter.
He was born on May 22, 1960, in Ube, Yamaguchi Prefecture, Japan. Anno
is best known for creating the seminal anime series "Neon Genesis
Evangelion," which first aired in 1995 and has since become a cultural
phenomenon. The series was widely acclaimed for its complex themes,
characters, and innovative use of visual effects.

Anno started his career in the anime industry in the early 1980s, working
as an animator on several projects. He went on to become a key figure in the industry, and was instrumental in creating several influential anime series, including "Nadia: The Secret of Blue Water" and "Royal Space Force: The Wings of Honneamise." In 1995, Anno created "Neon Genesis Evangelion," which became one of the most popular and acclaimed anime series of all time. The series dealt with complex themes such as depression, the human psyche, and religious and philosophical ideas, and was known for its use of psychological and spiritual elements. Its impact on the anime industry was immense. After the success of "Neon Genesis Evangelion," Anno continued to work in the anime industry, directing and producing other anime series and films. He is widely regarded as one of the most talented and influential figures in the anime industry and has received critical acclaim for his work.

Anno has famously said when asked by a student in an interesting "interview" like activity where he would be in a school, contributing to the children's curiculum, whether or not he "Liked the anime he made", to which he first responded, "Like.. Well I like some, but hate others". This was followed by the more famous dialouge of "What parts do you not like?",
to which he replied,
"The parts where I see myself".
