How Evangelion Delves Into The Human Condition

Sigmund Freud's psychoanalytic theory plays a significant role in the anime "Neon Genesis Evangelion" by Hideaki Anno. The series features several elements of Freudian psychology, including the Oedipal complex, the concepts of the id, ego, and superego, and the unconscious mind. The Oedipal complex, a central idea in Freudian theory, is represented in the relationship between the protagonist Shinji and his father Gendo.

In the series, Shinji struggles with feelings of jealousy and resentment towards his father, as well as feelings of guilt for his own perceived inadequacies. These emotions are a manifestation of the Oedipal complex, in which a son competes with his father for the affections of his mother. In the show, these emotions are complicated by the revelation that Shinji's mother died in childbirth and has been replaced by a series of clones.

The concepts of the id, ego, and superego are also central to the show. The id represents the primitive, instinctual desires of the unconscious mind, while the ego represents the rational, conscious mind. The superego represents the moral and ethical values of the individual. In the show, these concepts are represented through the characters' Evangelion robots, which symbolize their inner selves. The Evangelions, which are controlled by the pilots' unconscious minds, represent the id, while the rational, conscious pilots represent the ego. The tension between the id and the ego is a central theme in the series, as the characters struggle to reconcile their unconscious desires with their moral and ethical values. The idea of the unconscious mind is also central to the series. In Freudian theory, the unconscious mind is the repository of repressed memories, desires, and emotions that drive behavior. In the show, the characters' unconscious minds are represented by the Angels, who serve as both their inner demons and their unconscious desires. The characters struggle to come to terms with their unconscious minds and to reconcile their inner selves with their conscious selves. In conclusion, "Neon Genesis Evangelion" features several elements of Sigmund Freud's psychoanalytic theory, including the Oedipal complex, the concepts of the id, ego, and superego, and the unconscious mind. Through its exploration of these themes, the show offers a unique and insightful look at the human psyche and the psychological conflicts that drive human behavior.
